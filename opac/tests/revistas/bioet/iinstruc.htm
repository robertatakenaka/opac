Scope and Policy

The Revista Bioética was designed by the Conselho Federal de Medicina to foster multidisciplinary and pluralistic discussion of issues of bioethics and medical ethics. Its editorial line, as well as the composition and activities of the Editorial Board, are completely independent of the plenary session of the CFM. The authors are responsible for information in the articles, which do not necessarily express the official position of the Council.

To be accepted, the manuscripts need to be previously unpublished, conceptual or documentary in nature, resulting from research or experience in the field of bioethics or medical ethics, and critical review related to these themes. All manuscripts will be subjected to the scrutiny of the editors, of the Editorial Board and of ad hoc reviewers in a double-blind system, and must receive two approval reports.

Once received, the manuscript is checked regarding the size of the text (6,000 words) and abstract (150 words), formatted and checked for originality in the program plagiarism. At this stage the references are also verified to make sure they are complete, correctly numbered and presented in Vancouver style, in accordance with the editorial standards –which can be found at http://revistabioetica.cfm.org.br in Portuguese, English, and Spanish. 

If all these criteria are met, the manuscript goes to initial editing, where will be regarded as to the content, suitability for the editorial line, grammar and orthographical aspects, and given keywords in the database of Descritores em Ciências da Saúde – DeCS (descriptors of health sciences). If it is not necessary to make any changes to the manuscript at this stage, the next phase of the editorial process begins. If the need to amend or supplement in any of these respects is identified, the manuscript will return to authors for adjustments. Upon receipt of this initial rectified version, the phase of the ad hoc reviewers starts. If the amount or characteristic of the identified changes are considered significant, the manuscript may be rejected with guidance possible resubmission by meeting the given specifications.

In this second phase the advisers and ad hoc reviewers have 15 days after receiving the text to provide the review, which can be of approval; needs modifications; should be reconsidered after modifications; or not approved. The criteria considered in the assessments are: if it follows the editorial line, the originality of the ideas presented, if it is current, clarity of text, appropriateness of language, relevance of information, logical, conceptual and methodological consistency. Besides these issues, the title, abstract, indication and names of parts of the manuscript, an indication of the objectives, methodology, presentation of results, discussion and concluding remarks will also be subject to scrutiny. Furthermore, references are checked, considering their adequacy and currency.

The criterion for the selection of referees seeks to contemplate the broader analytical diversity possible, in line with the interdisciplinary field of bioethics. Each item is shipped with assessor training in specific job (specialties Health and Biology, Law, Philosophy, Social Sciences, etc.), or to another study that have discussed the issue (inequities in health, human rights, abortion, dysthanasia, genetics, research ethics, etc.) and also to take bioethicist trained lato or stricto sensu in Bioethics to evaluate the use of ethical concepts and categories. For final approval, it might be required to reformulate or adjust parts of the article, title or references, following the recommendations of the review. The authors have 20 to make the necessary changes.

In any of these steps the editors reserve the right to make normative, spelling and grammar alterations in texts, in order to maintain the formal standard of language and better understanding of the articles, respecting, however, the authors’ style. If the authors decide not to publish of the manuscript, after the initial editing, the version edited by the Revista Bioética belongs to it and cannot be sent to another journal. The final version of the manuscript will be submitted to the authors for approval. However, the final spelling and grammar review end of the article, the abstracts and the titles in English and Spanish by specialized companies, and also the final graphic editing, will not be sent to the authors.

Articles accepted for publication become property of the journal.

 

Form and  preparation of  manuscripts

  • Unpublished manuscripts in Portuguese, Spanish and English will be accepted. In each of the situations, the current orthographic rules of each language should be followed.
  • The article sent cannot have also been submitted to another periodical.
  • The opinions and concepts presented in the articles, and the origin and correctness of the citations are the authors’ responsibility. 
  • Will be summarily rejected manuscripts that reproduce in whole or in part, without due  reference, works of other authors, as well as an article or substantial part of it, already  published by the author.
  • The manuscripts should be accompanied by an initial page with the article’s title, the complete name of the author, their highest academic title, their departmental and institutional ties, besides the city, state and country.
  • As a separate item, the complete name of the first author, mail address and telephone number, for information exchange, and e-mail address of all the authors.
  • In case the first author is not the one responsible for the contact with the other authors in regards with the reviews and final approval of the paper, please specify the name, telephone number and e-mail address of the one responsible.
  • The individual collaborations of each author must be specified at the end.
  • If the work has been submitted to another journal and unpublished, must identify that vehicle.
  • The articles must be formatted in A4 size pages, Times New Roman font, size 12, 1.5 spacing, margins of 2.5cm, justified alignment. The titles must stand out in green color and be written in with only the first letter and names capitalized. There should not be paragraph entry of any other type of format that increases or decreases the distance between them. The pages must be numbered sequentially.
  • The text must have up to 6,000 words. The word limitation does not include the references, the identification of the paper and the abstract in the three languages.
  • The articles in Portuguese must have a concise abstract of 150 maximum, besides the translation to Spanish and English (up to 10 words), presented in the three languages.
  • Each abstract must be accompanied by minimum three maximum seven key-words, describing the content of the paper and that will help in the multiple indexation.
  • The key words should be taken from the Descriptors of Health Sciences (Descritores em Ciências da Saúde – DeCS) databank (http://www.bireme.br/php/decsws.php) and listed at the end of the summaries in the original language, in Spanish and in English, written with the first letter capitalized and separated by a period.
  • As a suggestion, the articles should be divided into sections, with titles and subtitles when necessary. Each of these parts and subcategories should stand out in green, never in a numerical progression. To explicit the subtitle inside a title, this should be in italics.
  • When an author is mentioned in the body of the text, put only the number of the reference at the end of the citation, in superscript, as the example: Potter 1.
  • Documents mentioned in the body of the text should be in italics (example: Declaration of Helsinki).
  • Works with footnotes will not be accepted. Any and all explanations or considerations should be inserted in the body of the text.

 

Send of the manuscripts

The manuscripts in Rich Text Format (RTF) must be submitted online at the link revistabioetica.cfm.org.br

The Revista Bioética considers that the presentation of the manuscript and submission of the same to the editors’ recommendations and of the Editorial Board characterizes the acceptance for publication. When the article is approved, all authors must send the authorization to publish the final version of the work by electronic means, the main author must also submit written in letter form, signed in his own hand, addressed to the Revista Bioética, as the template submitted by the editors. The journal should be cited in the event of reproduction of the article published in whole or in part, in any means of publication, printed or electronic.

The publication of research involving humans is the responsibility of the authors and should comply with the Standards and Guidelines for Research Involving Human Subjects (CNS Resolution 466/12), taking into consideration the principles of the Declaration of Helsinki, of the World Medical Association (1964 and subsequent reformulations prior to 2008), in addition to meeting the specific legislation of the country where the research was conducted. Research undertaken in Brazil must be accompanied by a copy of the approval document from the Ethics Research Committee (CEP).

The Revista Bioética, supports the of registration of clinical trials policies of the World Health Organization (WHO) and of the International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE), recognizing the importance of these initiatives for the registration and international publication in open access to information on clinical trials . Therefore, only research articles on clinical trials that have received an identification number to the Clinical Trial Registries validated by the criteria established by the WHO/ICMJE will be accepted, and the identification shall be submitted at the end of the summary.

Upon approval of research articles, authors should submit an affidavit regarding the content of the work, certifying even the absence of conflicts of interest that may have influenced the results.

There are no fees for submission and evaluation of articles.
