Scope and Policy

TEMA (São Carlos) publishes original works in Computational and Applied Mathematics. Articles should be complete mathematical papers and should contain relevant results or applications. In general, survey papers will only be published if previously comissioned by the Editorial Board.

Manuscripts are evaluated in a double-blind review process. Reviewers, invited by the Associate Editors, are specialists in the field of interest.

Accepted papers are edited and published online. The print version is freely distributed to SBMAC associates during the annual conferences or sent by post.

The Journal adopts CopySpider system for plagiarism identification.

Articles accepted for publication become the property Journal.

There are no fees for submission and review articles.

 

Form and preparation of manuscripts

TEMA (São Carlos) publishes original full papers in Computational and Applied Mathematics.

As part of the submission process, authors are required to check off their submission's compliance with all of the following items, and submissions may be returned to authors that do not adhere to these guidelines.

  1. Papers should be prepared, using LaTeX2e, in accordance with /TEMA.cls/ and /Modelo-TEMA/ available in: TEMA.zip.
    All submitted papers will have font size 10pt and printed area of 19,0cm by 12,7cm.
  2. Maximum number of pages in a submitted paper: 12.
    Figures and illustrations should not be in color. It is preferred that they are prepared in the “.eps” format.
  3. References should be prepared in alphabetical order and numbered.

 

Send of the manuscripts

Authors should submit the manuscripts through the journal website (http://www.sbmac.org.br/tema/seer/index.php/tema).
Registration and login are required to submit items online and to check the status of current submissions. - Access
Before submitting the manuscript, authors should verify the following instructions:

  1. Due to the peer review process, the instructions available in Ensuring blind peer review  should be followed.
    The article for evaluation must be submitted in ".pdf" format without author identification.
  2. The original ".tex" files of your manuscript and the figure files (if any) should be submitted as complementary documents.
At submission, the authors should suggest three names of possible reviewers, all related to the subject of the work submitted.
