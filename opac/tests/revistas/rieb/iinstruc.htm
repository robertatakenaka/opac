INSTRUCTIONS TO AUTHORS

  • General guidelines
  • Form and preparation of manuscripts

 

General guidelines

  • Revista do Instituto de Estudos Brasileiros publishes articles in Portuguese, Spanish, French, Italian and English.
  • Articles for appreciation and eventual publication in the Revista do Instituto de Estudos Brasileiros should be send in digital format to revistaieb@usp.br
  • Manuscripts will be peer-reviewed by two research professors, who will evaluate its authenticity and originality.

    • a. In case of divergence, a third evaluator will review the manuscript.
    b. The evaluators have 30 days to deliver their review.
    c. The final answer takes usually four months.

  • The periodic retains the right to adapt the manuscripts to its editorial project.
  • The authors undertake to submit formal authorization for publishing under the terms of Creative Commons BY-NC License (http://creativecommons.org).

Responsibilities

  • The authors undertake to inform anyone who might be interested in acquiring legal rights over the published texts about the Authorization Term signed for publication in the Revista do Instituto de Estudos Brasileiros.
  • Translations need formal authorization from the author(s) of the original work.
  • Authors have full responsibility for the reproduction of images on their articles.
  • The journal is not responsible for the contents or for the concepts expressed by the authors of the published articles.

The articles of the RIEB are evaluated by the blind peer review process, where they are submitted to the evaluation of two reviewers, considering the authenticity and originality of the work.

A. In the event of disagreement, a third opinion shall be heard.
B. The referees have 30 days to issue their opinions.
C. The average response time for the authors is four months.

Articles accepted for publication become the property of the journal.

 

Form and preparation of manuscripts

1. Formatting

    Manuscripts should be prepared using Word for Windows; A4 paper (297 × 210 mm); 2.5 cm margins on all sides; Times New Roman 12 pt font; 1.5 space throughout.

2. Number of Characters

  • Articles: between 30,000 and 52,000 characters (spaces included)
  • Reviews: between 5,000 and 20,000 characters (spaces included)
  • News and Documents: maximum of 20,000 characters (spaces included)

3. Additional information

  • The articles must follow ABNT NBR 6022/2003 standards.
  • The following information must appear on the front page and apart from the body of the text: title of the article, in Portuguese and in English; name(s) of the author(s); complete institutional affiliation (institution, department, unity, location, etc); brief summary of professional qualification.
  • If the work received financial support from an institution, the name of the referred institution must appear at the beginning of the text, under the author's name.
  • Abstract: maximum of 10 lines, in Portuguese and in English.
  • Keywords: 3 to 5, in Portuguese and in English.
  • Illustrations, graphs and tables must include respective legends.

4. Notes and bibliography

  • Explanatory and bibliographical footnotes must be numbered and obey ABNT NBR 6022/ 2003 standards. Samples:

    • 1 REIS FILHO, Nestor Goulart. A urbanização e o urbanismo na região das Minas. São Paulo: FAU/USP, 1999. (Cadernos do LAP, 30).

    2 HOLANDA, Sérgio Buarque de. O semeador e o ladrilhador. In: ________. Raízes do Brasil. São Paulo: Companhia das Letras, 1997. cap. 4, p. 93-138.

    3 TORRÃO FILHO, Amílcar. Paradigma do caos ou cidade da conversão?: a cidade colonial na América portuguesa e o caso da São Paulo na administração do Morgado de Mateus (1765-1775). 2004. 338 f. Dissertação (Mestrado em História) – Instituto de Filosofia e Ciências Humanas, Universidade Estadual de Campinas, 2004.

    4 BASTOS, Rodrigo Almeida. A arte do urbanismo conveniente: o decoro na implantação de novas povoações em Minas Gerais na primeira metade do século XVIII. In: PEREIRA, Sônia Gomes (Org.). Anais do VI Colóquio luso-brasileiro de história da arte. Rio de Janeiro: CBHA/UFRJ/UERJ/PUC-Rio, 2004. v. 2, p. 667-677.

    5 CANDIDO, Antonio. Dialética da malandragem. Revista do Instituto de Estudos Brasileiros, São Paulo, n. 8, p. 67-89, 1970.

    6 MAUAD, Ana Maria. Entre retratos e paisagens: modos de ver e representar no Brasil oitocentista. Studium, Campinas, v. 15, 2004. Disponível em: http://www.studium.iar.unicamp.br/15/01.html. Acesso em: 27 fev. 2007.

There are no fees for submission and evaluation of articles.
