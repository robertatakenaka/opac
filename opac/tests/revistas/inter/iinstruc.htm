Scope and policy

The editorial policy of INTER has as its principle, the registering and divulging of knowledge from the national and international scientific community. For this, it stimulates and develops interchange among researchers, teachers, students and professionals, who are active within the theme of local development, facilitating the approximation between scientific knowledge and the community. Another slant of the journal is to foment the interchange of information and experiences within the ambit of local development and related themes with other institutions, thus establishing a channel for the transmitting of studies and recent progress in the various areas of knowledge which converge on the themes in question.

The areas of interest of INTER are directed towards local development within the context of territoriality, which touch on micro and small undertakings: business chains, systems and local production arrangements, agro-forest systems and animal raising, productive chains, focusing on  processes which are interactive, cooperative, innovative and sustainable. Also of great interest to the journal are publications focusing on social-community dimensions with attention to traditional communities: the theory of social grouping, traditional communities (indigenous peoples, communities made up of the descendants of slaves, primitive fishermen, riverside populations, religious communities, amongst others), with a focus on their way of life, accumulated traditional knowledge and ways of territorial use for local development.

INTER publishes articles which can focus on local development, via theoretical reflections and reports of experiences, establishing a productive interaction between praxis and concepts (articles, interviews, reports, praxis-concepts). The summaries of dissertations are occasionally published which have been presented in the Post-Graduate Studies program in Local Development - an academic Master's Degree at the Dom Bosco Catholic University.

The evaluation of INTER is carried out semiannually by the members of the journal, by the Counsel for the Program of Post-Graduate Studies in Local Development, on which occasion the articles published are analyzed, with suggestions which bring about constant improvements in the journal.

The reading public of the journal are teachers, students, recent graduates, professionals in diverse areas of knowledge, business managers in public and private institutions, local, regional, national and international governing bodies. Also there is a system of exchange with other institutions.

POLICY AGAINST PLAGIARISM AND BAD CONDUCTS RESEARCH

Continuing our tradition of excellence, we report editorials improvements that aim to strengthen articles integrity published by this journal. In accordance with the COPE Guidelines (Committee on Publication Ethics), designed to encourage the identification of plagiarism, bad practices, fraud, possible ethics violations and opening process, we indicate:

 1. Authors should visit the website of the COPE http://publicationethics.org, which contains information for authors and publishers about ethics on research;

 2. Before submission, authors should attend the following criteria:
 - Articles containing data acquisition or analysis and data interpretation of other publications should be referred explicitly;
-  Articles containing a critical review of other author intellectual content, should be properly cited;
- All authors must attend the unprecedented criteria for article authorship and none of the researchers involved in the search may be omitted from the list of authors;
- The final approval article will be done by editors and editorial council.

3. To attend the criteria, the following procedures will be performed:
 Editors will evaluate manuscripts with CrossCheck System after submission, following the criteria:
1. Evaluation of the textual content of scientific articles searching for plagiarism identification, submissions that were already published and possible fraud in research;
2. After the results, The editors and editorial council will decide whether the article may or may not be sent to revision to at least three consultants the search knowledge area, educational institutions and / or National and foreign Research, of proven scientific production.
3. After the evaluation and possible suggestions, the article will be accepted if it has two favorable opinions and rejected when it has two unfavorable opinions

 

Criteria for publication

INSTRUCTIONS TO THE AUTHORS – CRITERIA FOR PUBLICATION INTERACTIONS JOURNAL

Art. 1st – Interactions, International Journal of the Local Development Program of the Dom Bosco Catholic University, aims at the publication of articles that, by their content, can contribute to the formation of researchers and to scientific development, as well as permitting the constant updating of knowledge in the specific area of Local Development.

Art. 2nd – The periodicity of the Journal will be, initially, semiannual, with the possibility of alteration in accordance with the necessities and demands of the program; the calendar for the publication of the Journal, as well as the closing date for each edition, will be equally defined by these necessities.

Art. 3rd – The publication of the studies should go through the supervision of an Editing Council made up of three professors of the Local Development Program of the Dom Bosco Catholic University, chosen by their colleagues.

Art. 4th – The evaluation of the studies for publication will be carried out by the International Editorial Council.

1st. Paragraph – The members of the International Editorial Council will be indicated by the teaching body of the Master’s Degree program in the Local Development, with a term of office valid for up to two years, of authorities with a recognized scientific production in national and international ambit.

2nd. Paragraph – The publication of articles is conditioned to a positive opinion, duly circumstantiated, registered by a member of the International Editorial Council.

3rd. Paragraph -  The Editorial Council, if necessary, will submit the articles to ad hoc consultants, for appreciation and opinion, in consequence of specifications in the areas of knowledge.

4th. Paragraph – The International Editorial Council will be able to propose to the Editing Council the adequacy of the presentation  procedures  for the studies, according to the specifications of each area.

Art. 5th – The Journal will publish studies of the following nature:

I – Unpublished articles,  which involve, in the form of studies, theoretical or practical approaches with reference to research in Local Development, and which present a relevant contribution to the thematic in question.

II – Translations of fundamental texts, that is, of those classical texts not available in the Portuguese Language, which constitute fundamentals of the specific area of Local Development and which, for this reason, contribute to the sustaining and density of academic reflection, with the necessary authorization of the author of the original text.

III – Unpublished interviews on relevant topics which are related to Local Development.

IV – Reports on original and relevant  works which can maintain the academic community informed on the advance of  reflections in the area of Local Development.

Art. 6th – The handing in of the original to the Journal should obey the following criteria:

I – The articles should obligatorily contain:

a) a title in Portuguese, English, Spanish and French;

b) the name or names of the author or authors, identified  in a footnote with their respective institutions, electronic addresses, data relevant to the production of the article, as well as possible institutional help;

c) each article should have a maximum of three authors who, by the simple submission of the article, assume responsibility as to the authorship and command of its content;

d) a summary in Portuguese, English, Spanish and French with, at the most six lines or four hundred letters, rigorously corrected and revised, accompanied, respectively, by key words, being always three, for the purpose of the indexation of the periodical;

e) the text with the necessary bibliographical references from the body of the actual text;

f) references.

II – The studies should be sent to acdorsa@ucdb.br and in the following format;

a) archive in  standard Microsoft Word;

b) digitalized authorization for publication (Art. 7th),  duly signed by the author or authors, as well as the complete address for correspondence, for the receiving of copies;

c) the text should have from 10 to 18 pages written with 1,5 spacing;

d) should the article have graphs, tables or photographs, the text  should be reduced because of the space the former  occupy;

e) the type face should be Times New Roman, number 12;

f) letter in italics should be reserved exclusively for the titles of publications and the words in another language from that used in  the text, eliminating also the use of underlined letters, those in bold type or  in capital letters; nevertheless, the subtitles of the article should be in bold type;

III – All the studies, in any language, should be elaborated and sent in three copies, with the text rigorously corrected and revised.

IV – Eventual illustrations, photos, images and tables with respective keys and captions should be contrasted and presented separately in TIFF, JPG, WMF or EPS format, with an indication, in the text, of the place where they should be inserted. All photographic material will be, preferably, in black and white.

V – The bibliographical references and cross references should be elaborated in accordance with the reference norms of the Brazilian Association of Technical Norms (ABNT – 6023).

VI – The opinions and concepts emitted by the authors of the articles are exclusively their own responsibility.

VII – The limits established for the diverse studies can only be exceeded in really exceptional cases, by suggestion of the International Editorial Council and the criterion of the  Editing Council.

Art. 7th – The author or authors should send their declaration of elaboration, command of the content and authorization for the publication of the article (available on the site of the periodical).

Art. 8th - Texts that do not rigorously obey the established criteria will not be accepted. The refused texts will be returned to the authors and accompanied by a justification.

Art. 9th – Simply by sending  texts, authorization for publication is implied  along with the gratuitous cession of  authorship rights.

Art. 10th – Authors who publish articles in Interactions will only be able to publish again after a period of two years.

Art. 11th – In the same number of the Journal, more than one article by the same author , even if in co-authorship,  will not be permitted.

Art. 12th– One  copy of the corresponding number of the Journal will be  sent, gratuitously, to the author whose work is approved for publication.

Art. 13th – Once the articles have been published, the Journal reserves all authorship rights, including those of translation, permitting, however, their posterior reproduction as a transcription, and with the required citation of origin.
