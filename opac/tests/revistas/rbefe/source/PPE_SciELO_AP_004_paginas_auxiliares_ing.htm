Basic information

The Revista Brasileira de Educação Física e Esporte / Brazilian Journal of Physical Education and Sport (RBEFE) is a quarterly publication of the School of Physical Education and Sport of the Universidade de São Paulo (EEFEUSP), which aims to publish research that contributes to the development knowledge about human movement related to the Physical Education and Sport. To achieve this goal, RBEF publishes original articles that bring scientific research results conducted at different levels of analysis, from the microscopic - for example, Biochemistry of Exercise - to the macroscopic - for example, Game Anthropology. It also publishes articles about studies that address topics relevant to professional contribution to the development of interventions in the areas of Physical Education and Sport - for instance, Pedagogy of Movement and Sports Training.

The abbreviation of the title is Rev. bras. educ. fís. esporte (Impr.), which should be used in bibliographies, footnotes, references and bibliographic strips.

INSTRUCTIONS TO AUTHORS

Editorial policy

REVISTA BRASILEIRA DE EDUCAÇÃO FÍSICA E ESPORTE REGULATIONS

The Revista Brasileira de Educação Física e Esporte is a publication of the School of Physical Education and Sport of the Universidade de Sao Paulo, and aims to publish research which contributes to the advancement of knowledge about human movement related to physical education, sports and related areas.

This standardization is intended to give researchers and reviewers greater flexibility in the submission process, review and publication of scientific articles. Please, notice that:

1. Will consider for publication original research under the condition that unique contributions to this journal, ie, that have not been, and will not be published elsewhere.

2. The submission of papers will be made only by means of uploading the web site. However, prior to submission, authors should go through a checklist verifying that all listed procedures were performed before the final submission.

3. Articles will be evaluated in two instances. In the first instance the director of RBEFE check the format, general aspects of writing and consistency of work. The board may either refuse the article in the first instance, if the work does not meet the quality standards of RBEFE, or request changes before accepting the submission. After submission, the article will be sent to two experts in the process of double blind. If there are conflicting opinions director RBEFE reserves the right to choose a tie for third reviewer or to decide on the acceptance or denial of the article.

4. At the time of submission the authors may request both two preferred reviewers and two non-preferred possible reviewers. However, the final decision on the choice of reviewers will be up to the editor of the Journal.

5. The submission of articles to the reviewers should also be done electronically. However, the reviewer should always acknowledge receipt of files in electronic format.

6. The reviewers should receive a form containing the main points that should be evaluated in the review process.

7. Reviewers will receive e-mails a week before the deadline for the submission of the review.

8. An archive of End Note is available online for the formatting of references.

9. The following types of articles may be submitted to RBEFE:

- Original articles in the areas:

1) Biomechanics of human movement

2) Pedagogy of human movement

3) Studies in Sport

4) Sociology of Physical Education and Sport

- Short reviews in areas 1, 2 and 3. However, Short Reviews are written by guest authors, or experts of renowned academic ability.

Each volume of RBEF will have only two (2) short reviews. The publication of these will be in accordance with the date of submission. The standards for review will be equal to the original articles, but the sections of materials and methods, results and discussion should be exchanged by the literature review.

10. From the time of receipt of suggestions from our reviewers, the authors will have a maximum of 60 days to make the suggested changes. If the return of the article happens after that period, the article will be considered as a new submission, and not as an article in the review process.

11. Articles may be sent in three languages: Portuguese, English and Spanish.

12. The authors are responsible for the content and writing of articles. Any errors are the sole responsibility of the same.

  1. 13. During the review process authors should include in the header item code and revision number (e.g.  05_ago-57 Revision 1).

Preparation of manuscripts

PREPARATION OF MANUSCRIPTS FOR

THE AREA OF BIODYNAMIC OF HUMAN MOVEMENT AND SPORT


General:


The author responsible for correspondence must be clearly defined. Manuscripts should be prepared in accordance with the standard style below. The editor reserves the right to adjust style to maintain the standard for uniformity. The authors are strongly encouraged to write in the most concise way possible due to publication costs.

The documents should contain 15 to 40 pages, including abstract, tables, figures and references. Should be sent an original and two complete copies, typed double-spaced, with 24 lines of 65 characters each. The paper format should be A4. The pages should contain line numbers, starting the count on each page.

The RBEFE requires that all research procedures are evaluated by an ethics committee and subjects signed an informed consent form before participation. The RBEF reserves the right to request approval form of the ethics committee in case of doubt in any experimental procedure. Studies involving experiments with animals must include a statement in the "Methods Section" that the experiments were conducted in accordance with local regulations.

The system of basic measures used in the Journal should be the "Système International d'Unités. As a general rule, should be used only standardized abbreviations and symbols. If nonstandard abbreviations are used, it is recommended their definition at the time of first appearance in the text.

The pages should be numbered at the top right corner, beginning with the title page and must be arranged in the following order: title page, summary page (including key words), text, abstract (including key words), references, titles and captions of tables and original artwork.

The figures must be numbered with Arabic numerals in the order they are inserted in the text and presented on separate sheets. The same procedure should be observed for tables.

Legends for figures and tables should be typed double-spaced on a separate page placed after the list of references. The position of each illustration or table should be indicated in the manuscript.

References: should follow the guidelines of the Brazilian Association of Technical Standards (ABNT), expressed in standard NB-66 (NBR 6023), displaying all authors (do not use "et alii or et al.). Put the titles of journals in full. See articles published in the Journal as examples.

Instructions for writing the sections of the work

Abstract: It should contain no more than three hundred words, and should not be structured (separating and highlighting, purposes, methods, results and conclusion). The keywords should not be contained in the title and should be at most 5.

Introduction: This section should develop the research hypothesis both accurately and carefully, leading to the purpose of the work. Clearly state the research hypothesis and purpose of the work at the end of the introduction. Its feature should not be a chapter, so the literature review must be made in the discussion at the time of the explanations and comparisons of the findings. The introduction must have no more than 7 concise paragraphs and not have sub-titles.

Methods: It is desirable that the authors begin this section with a generic explanation of the research design and, if possible, include a figure describing the design. The authors are encouraged to follow a structure of sub-topics presented below.

1. Participants: The sample characteristics should be defined and the conditions for controlling the main intervening variables described. Moreover, if the study involves humans, descriptive characteristics of the participants, which are not dependent variables, must be presented in the text (not include these data in tables). It is mandatory to explicit approval of the research protocol by the local ethics committee and that participants signed an informed consent before participation.

2. Procedures: All instruments and procedures used to collect data should be described in sufficient detail for reproduction purposes. If the study contains training protocols, or stimulation, a clear distinction must be made between the procedures for testing and training.

3. Statistical analysis: The authors should clearly describe the dependent variables and statistical tests. Factors and levels should also be described when appropriate. It is important to include the level of significance and measures of reproducibility of the tests.

Results: The authors should only describe the results that meet the purposes of the work. Additional data that are not related to the research problem should be disregarded. Make sure that figures and / or tables are cited in the text and that there is no duplication of information between tables and figures. It is advisable that the authors also show effect sizes to assist in the discussion of the results.

Discussion: This section should begin with a paragraph repeating the purposes of the study and reporting the main findings. Then, the results should be discussed in light of existing literature in an attempt to explain their research hypotheses and compare the findings with those of other studies. Do not include obvious statements like "more research is needed ...".
